Privacy Policy — Night Field & Fireflies

Last updated: March 23, 2025

Extension: Night Field & Fireflies (Chrome extension)
Source: github.com/sharmark9931/fireflies-extension

Summary

This extension does not collect, sell, or transmit your personal data to us. It runs mostly on your device. Features that use Chrome permissions only access what is needed for the new tab experience described below, and that data stays on your computer unless you choose to visit third-party websites (for example, Google).

What this extension does

  • Replaces your new tab page with a themed scene (fireflies / butterflies, grass animation).
  • Optionally shows a Google search box, a Google apps launcher, and a top sites strip built from Chrome’s list of frequently visited pages.

Data we do not collect

We do not:

  • Run analytics or tracking in the extension.
  • Operate a server that receives your browsing data, searches, or identity.
  • Sell or share personal data.

The extension is distributed as open source; you can review the code on GitHub.

Chrome permissions and how they are used

Permission Why it exists
tabs Used only to open URLs in new tabs when you click items in the Google apps menu or top sites dock. We do not read the content of your tabs, history, or passwords.
topSites Used only to read Chrome’s locally stored list of most visited URLs and titles so the “Top sites” dock can show shortcuts. This data is not sent to our servers—we do not operate a backend for this extension.

Third-party services

If you use the search box or open Google services, those requests are handled by Google under Google’s Privacy Policy. The new tab may load public images (favicons, product artwork) over HTTPS from Google or other hosts under their normal policies.

Local storage

The extension may store preferences on your device (for example, UI visibility toggles) using browser storage. This data stays on your device and is not synced to us.

Children’s privacy

This extension is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes

We may update this policy if the extension’s behavior changes. Updates will be published in the GitHub repository.

Contact

For privacy questions, open an issue on the GitHub repository.
